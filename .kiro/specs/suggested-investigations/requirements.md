# Requirements Document

## Introduction

The Suggested Investigations feature extends the Research Analyst Platform with automatic research lead generation. After a case file's data has been ingested and indexed, the platform analyzes the Neptune knowledge graph to identify the most promising areas of investigation and presents them as a ranked "Top N Research Leads" list on the Case Detail page. Each suggestion includes a natural-language explanation of why the lead is interesting, generated by Bedrock, and a one-click action to drill down into a sub-case. Suggestions are cached to avoid recomputation and can be dismissed by the analyst.

This feature builds on the existing Pattern Discovery (Requirement 3) and Drill-Down Investigation (Requirement 4) capabilities, adding a proactive, automated layer that surfaces high-value leads without requiring the analyst to manually trigger pattern discovery.

## Glossary

- **Platform**: The Research Analyst Platform — the complete serverless system including all backend services, AI components, and frontend interface
- **Case_File**: A logical research container representing a single research topic, stored as a metadata record in Aurora
- **Sub_Case_File**: A Case_File created by drilling down into a specific pattern or entity discovered within a parent Case_File
- **Graph**: The Neptune Serverless graph database storing Entities as nodes and their relationships as edges
- **Suggestion**: A single research lead identified by automated graph analysis, containing a title, ranked score, signal source, involved entities, and a Bedrock-generated explanation
- **Suggestion_Set**: The complete ordered collection of Suggestions generated for a Case_File, stored in Aurora as a cached result
- **Signal**: One of three graph analysis metrics used to identify interesting research leads: Entity_Connection_Count, Community_Detection, or Betweenness_Centrality
- **Entity_Connection_Count**: A Signal measuring the total number of edges (relationships) connected to an Entity node in the case subgraph — entities with high connection counts are referenced across many documents and relationships
- **Community_Detection**: A Signal identifying clusters of tightly connected Entity nodes that form natural research topics — detected via connected component traversal in the case subgraph
- **Betweenness_Centrality**: A Signal measuring how often an Entity node lies on the shortest path between other nodes — entities with high betweenness centrality bridge otherwise disconnected topic groups and represent hidden connections
- **Research_Interface**: The Streamlit-based frontend for interacting with case files
- **Ingestion_Pipeline**: The Step Functions orchestration that processes raw data through extraction, vectorization, and graph population stages

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Automatic Suggestion Generation After Indexing

**User Story:** As a research analyst, I want the platform to automatically identify interesting research leads after my case file data is indexed, so that I can immediately see the most promising areas to investigate without manually running pattern discovery.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Ingestion_Pipeline sets a Case_File status to "indexed", THE Platform SHALL automatically trigger Suggestion generation for that Case_File
2. WHEN Suggestion generation is triggered, THE Platform SHALL analyze the Case_File's Neptune subgraph using three Signals: Entity_Connection_Count, Community_Detection, and Betweenness_Centrality
3. WHEN Entity_Connection_Count analysis runs, THE Platform SHALL query the Graph for all Entity nodes in the Case_File subgraph and rank them by total edge count (both inbound and outbound relationships)
4. WHEN Community_Detection analysis runs, THE Platform SHALL traverse the Case_File subgraph to identify connected components of Entity nodes and rank communities by member count and internal edge density
5. WHEN Betweenness_Centrality analysis runs, THE Platform SHALL compute approximate betweenness centrality for Entity nodes in the Case_File subgraph and rank entities by centrality score
6. WHEN all three Signal analyses complete, THE Platform SHALL combine and deduplicate results into a single ranked list of Suggestions, ordered by a composite score derived from the three Signals
7. IF the Case_File subgraph contains fewer than two Entity nodes, THEN THE Platform SHALL produce an empty Suggestion_Set and record a message indicating insufficient data for Suggestion generation

### Requirement 2: Bedrock-Generated Explanations

**User Story:** As a research analyst, I want each suggested investigation to include a plain-language explanation of why it's interesting, so that I can quickly assess whether a lead is worth pursuing.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a Suggestion is generated, THE Platform SHALL call Bedrock to produce a natural-language explanation describing why the identified entities or community form an interesting research lead
2. THE Platform SHALL include in each Bedrock explanation prompt the involved Entity names, Entity types, the Signal source (Entity_Connection_Count, Community_Detection, or Betweenness_Centrality), and the composite score
3. WHEN a Suggestion is based on Community_Detection, THE Platform SHALL include the community member names and the number of internal relationships in the explanation prompt
4. WHEN a Suggestion is based on Betweenness_Centrality, THE Platform SHALL include the names of the topic groups that the bridging entity connects in the explanation prompt
5. IF Bedrock fails to generate an explanation, THEN THE Platform SHALL use a fallback explanation containing the Entity names, Signal source, and composite score in a structured format

### Requirement 3: Suggestion Display on Case Detail Page

**User Story:** As a research analyst, I want to see suggested investigations on the Case Detail page, so that I have immediate visibility into the most promising research leads when reviewing a case file.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a Case_File has a Suggestion_Set, THE Research_Interface SHALL display a "Suggested Investigations" section on the Case Detail page below the case metadata
2. THE Research_Interface SHALL display each Suggestion with a rank number, title (derived from the primary Entity or community name), the Signal source label, the composite score, and the Bedrock-generated explanation
3. THE Research_Interface SHALL display the top N Suggestions where N is configurable per Case_File with a default value of 10
4. WHEN a Case_File has no Suggestion_Set, THE Research_Interface SHALL display a message indicating that suggestions are not yet available
5. WHEN a Case_File has an empty Suggestion_Set, THE Research_Interface SHALL display a message indicating insufficient data for suggestions

### Requirement 4: One-Click Sub-Case Creation from Suggestions

**User Story:** As a research analyst, I want to create a focused sub-investigation from any suggested lead with a single click, so that I can quickly drill down into promising topics.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Research_Interface SHALL display a "Create Sub-Case" button for each Suggestion in the Suggested Investigations section
2. WHEN the analyst clicks "Create Sub-Case" on a Suggestion, THE Platform SHALL create a Sub_Case_File using the existing drill-down mechanism (Requirement 4 of the base platform) with the Suggestion's involved entities as the drill-down focus
3. WHEN a Sub_Case_File is created from a Suggestion, THE Platform SHALL pre-populate the Sub_Case_File topic name with the Suggestion title and the description with the Bedrock-generated explanation
4. WHEN a Sub_Case_File is successfully created from a Suggestion, THE Research_Interface SHALL display a confirmation with a link to the new Sub_Case_File

### Requirement 5: Suggestion Caching and Storage

**User Story:** As a research analyst, I want suggestions to load instantly when I view a case file, so that I don't have to wait for recomputation on every page load.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a Suggestion_Set is generated, THE Platform SHALL store the Suggestion_Set in Aurora linked to the Case_File ID, including all Suggestion details, generation timestamp, and the configuration (N value) used
2. WHEN the Case Detail page loads and a cached Suggestion_Set exists for the Case_File, THE Platform SHALL retrieve and display the cached Suggestion_Set without recomputing
3. WHEN the analyst triggers a new ingestion on a Case_File that already has a Suggestion_Set, THE Platform SHALL regenerate the Suggestion_Set after the new ingestion completes to reflect updated graph data
4. THE Platform SHALL include the generation timestamp in the displayed Suggestion_Set so the analyst can see when suggestions were last computed

### Requirement 6: Suggestion Dismissal

**User Story:** As a research analyst, I want to dismiss suggestions that are not relevant to my investigation, so that the list stays focused on leads I care about.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Research_Interface SHALL display a "Dismiss" button for each Suggestion in the Suggested Investigations section
2. WHEN the analyst clicks "Dismiss" on a Suggestion, THE Platform SHALL mark that Suggestion as dismissed in Aurora and remove the Suggestion from the displayed list
3. WHEN a Suggestion_Set is displayed, THE Platform SHALL exclude all previously dismissed Suggestions from the visible list
4. WHEN a Suggestion_Set is regenerated after new ingestion, THE Platform SHALL retain dismissal records for Suggestions that match previously dismissed entities, so that the analyst does not see the same dismissed leads again
5. IF all Suggestions in a Suggestion_Set are dismissed, THEN THE Research_Interface SHALL display a message indicating that all suggestions have been reviewed
