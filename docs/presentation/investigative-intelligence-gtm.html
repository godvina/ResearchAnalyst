The Two Challenges Defining Federal Investigations

🏛️

Challenge 1: Do More With Less

Federal workforce reductions mean fewer investigators handling the same — or growing — caseload. Experienced analysts leave with no backfills. Institutional knowledge walks out the door.

The Compounding Effect
  • 📉 Workforce reductions with no new backfills
  • 📚 Thousands of cases queued — throughput must increase, not decrease
  • ⏱️ Investigators spend 60–80% of time on manual document review
  • 📅 Cases take 18–24 months average — too slow for the current pace
  • 📝 Brief preparation is manual, repetitive, and error-prone
500TB+

of case evidence per major federal investigation

🔍

Challenge 2: Find the Needle You Don't Know Exists

The hardest cases aren't about finding a known document — they're about discovering connections, patterns, and anomalies that no one knew to look for. The evidence that cracks the case is buried in 500TB of data, invisible to keyword search.

What's Missing Today
  • 🚫 No AI-assisted pattern discovery across evidence
  • 🔒 Evidence silos across agencies — connections invisible
  • 🕸️ No knowledge graph linking entities across cases
  • 📦 Legacy tools (Relativity, Nuix) find documents, not intelligence
  • 🎯 No way to assess prosecution readiness until months into a case
Antitrust: The Data Challenge
Millions of corporate communications, financial records, market data, pricing spreadsheets, and board minutes — the smoking gun is a pattern across data types, not a single document.

AI doesn't replace investigators — it gives each one the throughput of ten.

Risks and Mitigation

RISK 1

Service teams know 1 of 3 services. Neptune SA doesn't know OpenSearch. Bedrock SA doesn't know Neptune.

MITIGATION

Cross-train SAs across Neptune / OpenSearch / Bedrock. Sell as a solution with Kiro, not individual services.

RISK 2

Partners / ProServe lack solution expertise across all three services.

MITIGATION

Solution blueprints + Kiro-assisted deployment. Reduce dependency on deep multi-service expertise.

RISK 3

OpenSearch Batch Loader not in GovCloud / FedRAMP yet.

MITIGATION

Service team builds interim pipeline code. DOJ becomes beta customer with requirements flowing to service team. One-time build mimics future Batch Loader.

Every risk has a clear mitigation path. The biggest risk is doing nothing — competitors are building this.
